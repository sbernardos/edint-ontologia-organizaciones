The Spanish Public Organizations Ontology: Description back to ToC

Figure 1 shows the main classes of the ontology and their properties, although it only includes the names of those that are specific to the ontology. As can be seen, this is an extension of the Organization Ontology of W3C to represent particular aspects of public administration organizations in Spain. To this end, a subtype of organization, called organization too, has been created. This organization has a competence region, which is an administrative region of Spain (autonomous community, province, county, district, municipality).

Organizations have properties to represent their external identifier, the legal provision that regulates the organization, their hierarchical level within the organization to which they belong, and notes regarding their deregistration, if it has occurred. In addition, they have a status, correspond to a type, and are located at an administration level, which are represented by SKOS concepts, with the corresponding taxonomy.

A subclass of organizational unit called organic unit has also been created to represent organizational units that belong to a public law entity, which is a subclass of formal organization created to meet needs of general interest and whose activity depends on public funding or control.

Finally, the facilities of the infrastructure ontology have been considered as a subclass of site.
